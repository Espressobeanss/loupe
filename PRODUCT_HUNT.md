## First comment (your maker comment — the story sells it)

Hey Hunters 👋

I kept watching small teams sit on real user signal — a pile of support tickets, a
Figma flow, a few call notes — but never turn it into a decision. No time to compile
it, no senior designer to critique it.

So I built **Loupe**. You throw the mess in, and it gives you one report that reads
as a single argument: here's the verdict, here's what we saw, here's *why* it's
broken, here's what to do. Every recommendation traces back to a raw source — no
black box.

Three things I cared about:
- **It has an opinion.** The verdict is one falsifiable sentence with a confidence
  score, not a hedge.
- **It critiques, not summarizes.** The core is a critic *speaking* — naming the
  heuristic you broke — the design crit you'd otherwise convene a room for.
- **It adapts to who's reading.** Same diagnosis, different actions for a founder vs.
  a PM vs. ops — with a hand-off when the fix isn't yours to make.

It's open source (MIT), runs with zero dependencies, and the AI engine is a clean
seam you can wire your own model into. Would love your honest crit — fitting, given
what it does. What would you throw at it first?

— Yilan
